What to wear

Men should wear a suit. We're not expecting full black-tie, but smartly dressed with a nice shirt, jacket & accessory (cravat, tie, bowtie, pocket square).

The ladies attending should wear something relatively formal but sun-friendly.

Note that the ceremony takes place in the evening and will be outdoors. In the unlikely event in bad weather, we'll be in a nice space next to the green we'd be at anyway. While the weather is typically quite warm (compared to Ireland, anyway!) the evening will get cool as time passes. That said, we will be moving indoors at midnight. Also note that the colour scheme for the wedding is purple and yellow. Though you probably guessed that from the site.

List of things to do!

Siam park - Famous water park in Adeje. Huge place that everyone raves about. Except Kevin, because he can't swim.

Volcano El Teide - Tenerife, like all Canary islands, is a volcanic island. El Teide is the volcano located in Tenerife & there are daily tours up & around it.

Mustcat Catamarans - You can go with any Catamaran company, but these are highly rated. They take you out into the water for a few hours where you can see crazy wildlife and get sunburned.

Bars & nightlife - Primary strips of bars can be found in Las Americas, but there's loads everywhere. Remember, the entire island is purely there for tourism! And yes, there's loads of Irish bars.
